INTRODUCTION

Welcome to the Developer Introduction (DEV I) Program

Developer Introduction is a 10-week training program. The 10 weeks include self-study time, reviews, labs, booster quizzes, a midterm, and a final all to make sure you are retaining the information. The main goal of DEV I is to teach you the foundations of the C# programming language. Upon successfully completing DEV I, you will graduate into the Developer Experience (Dev X) program.

Congratulations on being selected to participate in DEV I! Today is the first step in your journey to becoming an application developer and your Information Technology career.  As the DEV I program gets going, you will be challenged, you will improve and you will grow. The journey will not be easy and at times you may feel overwhelmed.  Know that you are 100% supported by your leaders, trainers and mentors.

This document will:

  • Explain what the program is and the purpose behind it
  • Summarize the schedule and the evaluation process
  • Recognize the expectations of the program

STRUCTURE

Program Structure

This section breaks down the flow of the training and what to expect weekly.

Self-Study

Each week are will be given a pre-training assignment (PTA) that will cover that weeks topic. These PTA's will come in either an interactive Academy item or as an electronic PDF. It is your responsibility to use the self-study time to complete these assignments and any supplemental resources

Make-Up Sessions

Monday mornings are also a great opportunity to make up any session you may have missed previously due to an absence

Workbook

In an effort to keep your skills sharp and to offer you varying challenges. The workbook will be filled with exercises for you to tackle during this time as well

Pre-Training Assignments (Graded)

In the realm of software development, the number one metric for achieving confidence and improving your skills is to accomplish as many “hands-on” activities as possible. The more you use the tools you will need as a developer, the greater your comfort will be when it comes time to use these tools. The more lines of actual code you write implementing the concepts taught to you, the more confident you will be in your abilities when you become a full-time developer.

Since your time for classroom training is limited in this program, it is of utmost importance that the majority of that time is spent practicing developer skills and doing activities that strengthen your comprehension of the concepts. To ensure we provide you with the hands-on time you need, the amount of lecture time you will receive will be minimal. However, we need to make sure you have knowledge and basic comprehension of the terms and theories behind the practices. This is the purpose of the pre-training assignment.

By providing you with “homework” before you show up for the training, we are giving you the opportunity to research the concepts on your own. This allows you to formulate your own thoughts and ideas on the subject as well as draft any questions you would like answered for clarification. You will be provided with ample time to complete the assignment, as well as given a list of potential resources to find the required information. However, you have the freedom to select and identify other resources. The key point is that you express the answers in your own terms.

Each course has a pre-training assignment associated with it. There is time allotted within the schedule for you to work on the pre-training assignments.

Keep in mind that a trainer will be available to you during the pre-training assignment time allotted in your schedule.

Course Reviews

Course reviews follow the pre-training assignments and are designed to reinforce the material covered in the pre-training assignments. These sessions are led by the facilitator and include various activities to really drive the content home.

Course Labs

The labs are designed to give you the opportunity to apply the concepts we discuss in pre-training assignments and the review in code.  You will be given a set of requirements and your job is to find a solution for each lab exercise.

While it will provide you with examples of how a particular section should be constructed or a code block might look, it will not provide the exact code or all the specific details for your implementation. It is expected that you will find a way to accomplish the lab requirements using the tools available to you. Don’t forget, we’re here to help!

We will spend time, in class, exploring the various solutions developed by you and your DEV I peers.

Supplemental Instructor Led Trainings

These sessions are 2 - 3 hour training's led by the instructor on specific tools and/or resources you should utilize when coding. The difference with these types of training in comparison to the other training's you will receive is that these will not have a separate session for Reviews and Labs. Instead, we will spend the entire session going over the concepts and applying them throughout the duration.

Quiz (Graded)

Booster quizzes are given on the Tuesday of every week. These fill-in-the-blank / multiple choice questions allow you to see how much information you were able to retain from the previous week's topic.

Putting It All Together

Each week we will take everything we have learned up until that point, and spend an afternoon building applications that bring all the pieces together.

Practical Assessments (Graded)

Practical assessments are similar to labs, but graded. You will be given a set of requirements and then a duration of time to complete as much as you can. This gives you the opportunity to demonstrate what skills you gained throughout that week of training. Each practical assessment will focus on that weeks topic but will have pieces of skills acquired from previous week's sessions.

Midterm Project (Graded)

The midterm project will be a set of requirements to build out a Console application that utilizes all the topics we have covered up until this point. Your final product will be presented on the last day of this week.

Final Project (Graded)

The final project will be a set of requirements to build out a Console application that utilizes all the topics we have covered throughout the entire program. Your final product will be presented on the last day of this week.

Retrospectives and 1-on-1's

Every week you will be given a chance to provide feedback on the training sessions, program, and trainers. Retrospective sessions (Retros) will be facilitated to gather your feedback in hopes of improving future iterations.

In addition to the Retro's, your team leader will be meeting with you 1-on-1 every two weeks. The purpose of these 1-on-1's is to provide you with your progress in the program and ensure that you have the opportunity to discuss any concerns you may have.
